### MMR Terminal

I built the **MMR Terminal** as an extension of this research.

Its purpose is simple: to quantify **Market Maker Risk (MMR)** through the volatility implied by option prices.

Rather than treating implied volatility as just another market statistic, the framework interprets it as a measure of the risk market makers must assume when quoting and hedging options. This provides a market-maker-centric view of expected volatility, risk perception, and option pricing distortions.

The terminal is intended for advanced option traders, quantitative researchers, and derivatives professionals. To make practical use of its outputs, users should have a sound understanding of:

- Black-Scholes option pricing
- Implied volatility
- Put-call parity
- Option Greeks
- Option market structure

For traders who wish to understand the underlying mathematical framework and its practical application, I also conduct a structured training program covering the theory, quantitative concepts, and real-market implementation behind the model.
